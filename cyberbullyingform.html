Cyberbullying is a type of harassment that is carried out through electronic communication, such as social media, text messages, or email. It involves the use of technology to deliberately and repeatedly intimidate, humiliate, or threaten an individual or a group. Cyberbullying can take many forms, such as spreading rumors or lies, posting hurtful or embarrassing messages or images, or creating fake accounts to impersonate or harass someone. The effects of cyberbullying can be devastating and can include anxiety, depression, social isolation, and in extreme cases, even suicide.
If you believe you are a victim of cyberbullying, it is important to seek help from trusted adults, such as parents or teachers, and to report the incident to the authorities or relevant online platform.
