Device hacking is the unauthorized access and control of a victim's computer, laptop, mobile phone, or any other electronic device. Hackers can gain access to a device by exploiting vulnerabilities in its software or by tricking the user into installing malicious software, such as a virus or spyware. Once a hacker gains access to a device, they can steal sensitive information, install additional malware, or even take control of the device remotely.
If you suspect that your device has been hacked, it is important to seek professional assistance from cybersecurity experts to mitigate the damage and prevent further unauthorized access.
